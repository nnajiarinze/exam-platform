# ADR-010: Knowledge-first content model

## Status

Accepted

## Context

Treating questions as truth makes factual correction, sourcing, variant generation, and impact analysis unreliable.

## Decision

Knowledge facts are canonical and traceable to reliable sources. Questions are derived assessment artifacts: every question version links to a learning objective, fact version, and source reference. Content changes can identify affected questions.

## Consequences

### Positive

Source traceability, reusable facts, safer variants/translations, and targeted correction workflows.

### Negative

Editorial modeling and tooling are more involved than a flat question bank.

## Alternatives considered

A question-first bank is fast to start but duplicates facts and obscures provenance. Source-only pages lack assessment structure.

## Revisit conditions

Extend fact relationships or provenance standards when editorial evidence requires it; do not make questions canonical without a replacement traceability model.
